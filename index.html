// 01 — About

Bridging materials, heat transfer, and system design.

SK
Soonwook Kim
Assistant Professor
Principal Investigator
Google Scholar ORCID CV Email

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at Kyung Hee University. My research lies at the intersection of heat transfer, materials engineering, and system design, with a focus on managing transient and high heat-flux conditions in modern thermal systems.

My work explores the integration of phase change materials (PCMs) into cooling architectures to address dynamic thermal loads that cannot be effectively managed by conventional steady-state approaches. I am particularly interested in bridging the gap between material behavior and system-level performance, enabling scalable and reliable thermal solutions.

Prior to joining Kyung Hee University, I received my Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering from the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign. My doctoral research focused on advanced cooling strategies for electronics, including PCM-based thermal buffering and hybrid cooling systems.

// 02 — Research

Four thrusts. One mission: tame the heat.

Traditional cooling approaches struggle to handle transient thermal spikes. We address this challenge by combining phase change materials, advanced cooling architectures, and predictive modeling frameworks.

THRUST 01
🔥

Transient Thermal Management with PCM

We investigate how phase change materials buffer transient heat loads in electronics and energy systems, addressing low thermal conductivity and degradation during melting.

phase-change buffering conductivity
THRUST 02
💧

Hybrid Cooling Systems

We develop integrated architectures that combine PCMs with liquid cooling to achieve both peak load buffering and efficient steady-state heat removal.

liquid-cooling PCM data-center
THRUST 03
⚡

Pressure-Tunable Thermal Energy Storage

We explore pressure-driven phase transitions to enable tunable thermal energy storage and a new class of cooling concepts based on caloric effects.

caloric pressure storage
THRUST 04
🧠

Data-Driven Thermal Design

We develop reduced-order and machine learning–based models to enable fast prediction and optimization of thermal systems across operating regimes.
