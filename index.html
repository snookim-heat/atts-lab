// 01 — About

Bridging materials, heat transfer, and system design.

SK
Soonwook Kim
Assistant Professor
Principal Investigator
Google Scholar ORCID CV Email

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at Kyung Hee University. My research lies at the intersection of heat transfer, materials engineering, and system design, with a focus on managing transient and high heat-flux conditions in modern thermal systems.

My work explores the integration of phase change materials (PCMs) into cooling architectures to address dynamic thermal loads that cannot be effectively managed by conventional steady-state approaches. I am particularly interested in bridging the gap between material behavior and system-level performance, enabling scalable and reliable thermal solutions.

Prior to joining Kyung Hee University, I received my Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering from the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign. My doctoral research focused on advanced cooling strategies for electronics, including PCM-based thermal buffering and hybrid cooling systems.

// 02 — Research

Integrated thermal management through three research thrusts.

Our research integrates material innovation with system co-design to address the growing thermal challenges in modern electronics. Three complementary thrusts work in tandem — from AI-driven design optimization to next-generation cooling solutions and system-level integration strategies.

// Lab Vision

Integrated Thermal Management via Material Innovation & System Co-Design

We bridge the gap between novel thermal materials and real-world system requirements, enabling cooling solutions that scale with the increasing power densities of modern electronics, data centers, and power systems.

THRUST 01
🤖

AI-enabled Thermal System Design

We develop machine learning–based and reduced-order models to accelerate the design and optimization of complex thermal systems. By combining physics-informed neural networks with experimental data, we enable rapid evaluation of design candidates that would be prohibitively expensive to assess through traditional CFD or experiments alone.

machine-learning PINN reduced-order-model design-optimization
THRUST 02
🌿

Next-generation Cooling Solution Development

We investigate novel cooling mechanisms beyond conventional heat sinks — including phase change materials, two-phase cooling, and pressure-tunable thermal energy storage — to handle transient and high heat-flux loads that conventional steady-state systems cannot manage.

PCM two-phase caloric transient
THRUST 03
📦

System & Packaging-level Cooling Strategy Integration

We design cooling architectures that integrate seamlessly with electronic packaging — from chip-level thermal interfaces to module- and system-level heat removal pathways. This holistic approach ensures that material-level innovations translate into real performance gains at the device and system level.
